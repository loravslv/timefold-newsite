What trust actually requires

Four things planning software has to deliver before anyone will rely on it for their day's work.



Correct & feasible

80% feasible is 100% useless. Hard rules - legal limits, skills, working time - never broken.



Explainable

A reason behind every assignment, every tradeoff, every exception. No black box. 



Honest about failure

When no feasible plan exists, say so - and which constraint, resource, or input led there. 



Adaptable

Planners can pin, override, and propose changes - and instantly see how the plan responds. 

The toolkit

What the platform gives you to deliver on the four.  The surfaces planners and developers actually use during a rollout.

Score analysis

A constraint-by-constraint breakdown of where the plan loses or earns score. Justifications exportable for stakeholder review.
Real world metrics

Business metrics in the language of the operation - mileage, unassigned visits, preferences honored - and how much they improved.
Plan visualization

Built-in views per model. Calendars for shifts, maps for routes, Gantt for tasks. For planners to spot-check, not for distribution to end users.
Pinning

Lock the assignments that can't move and the solver optimizes everything else around them.
Change impact

Propose a swap, see immediately whether it's feasible and how it shifts the plan's metrics. No guessing.
